 Ms O'Keeffe replaces Mr Les Douglas, who has been Ambassador since  December 1989.

 Australia and Nepal enjoy a warm and friendly relationship which predates the  establishment of the Australian Embassy in Kathmandu in 1984. Australia's  development assistance has contributed, both bilaterally and multilaterally, to  forestry regeneration, civil aviation, human resources, hydroelectricity and grain 

 management programs in Nepal. Tourism from Australia to Nepal is also a  major item in the bilateral economic relationship.

 Since 1991, Ms O'Keeffe has been Regional Director, Queensland and the  Northern Territory, for the Australian International Development Assistance  Bureau (AIDAB). From 1989 to 1991 she was Director, Refugee and Disaster  Coordination at AIDAB, and from 1986 to 1989 Director of Public Affairs, also at  AIDAB. Previous positions have been with the Federal Department of 

 Transport and the International Committee of the Red Cross in Bangkok and  Geneva. Ms O'Keeffe has also been a journalist with the Glen Innes Examiner,  the Zimbabwe Herald and the Croydon Advertiser (London).

 Ms O'Keeffe is expected to take up her appointment in July.
