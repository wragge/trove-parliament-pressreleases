 EMPOWERING MULTICULTURAL COMMUNITIES IN BENNELONG    

 Prime Minister Kevin Rudd joined the Labor Candidate for Bennelong, Jason Yat-sen Li, today to meet with Bennelong residents and award the Ryde Uniting  Church a grant as part of Federal Labor’s successful Building Multicultural  Communities Program.     

 The Prime Minister and Minister for Multicultural Affairs Kate Lundy met people  from migrant and refugee backgrounds who will amongst those benefitting from  the English learning computer facilities at the Ryde Uniting Church.    

 Ryde Uniting Church will receive $5,000 to purchase new computers and  furniture to support students in its ‘Little Learners’ and ‘English Conversation  Class’ programs for young migrant and refugee children. The classes help  students improve their English skills so they can attend local primary schools.    

 This $5,000 grant forms part of the Rudd Labor Government’s Building  Multicultural Communities Program which will strengthen social cohesion and  promote inclusion in diverse communities.    

 The Prime Minister said Australia’s cultural diversity is one of the nation’s  greatest strengths and Federal Labor is proud to support organisations working  in communities to promote cohesion and inclusiveness.    

 The Prime Minister was welcomed by the Labor Candidate for Bennelong, Jason  Yat-sen Li, to meet residents and talk about how this funding will support the  diverse communities in this area.    

 Funding for this commitment is already included in the Budget.
