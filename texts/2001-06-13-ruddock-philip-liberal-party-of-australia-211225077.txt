 News Room

 Reappointments to the MRT and RRT MPS 074/2001

 The Minister for Immigration and Multicultural Affairs, Philip Ruddock, today announced the reappointment for three years of a number of members of the Migration Review Tribunal (MRT), and the Refugee Review Tribunal (RRT).

 Thirty six members have been reappointed to the MRT (comprising seven full-time members and twenty-nine part-time members) and fifty four members to the RRT, (comprising three senior members, thirty-one full-time members and twenty part-time members. The new appointments start on 1 July 2001.

 The MRT provides independent merits review of general migration decisions and the RRT is responsible for providing independent merits review of decisions to refuse or cancel protection visas made by the Department of Immigration and Multicultural Affairs.

 "Members being reappointed have demonstrated that they are able to quickly produce sound decisions that are consistent with accepted legal standards.

 Their reappointment will also ensure that the wealth of knowledge and experience that they possess remains with the Tribunals they represent," Mr Ruddock said.

 Mr Ruddock also announced the appointment of Mr Steve Karas OAM to head both tribunals from 1 July 2001. See separate media release.
