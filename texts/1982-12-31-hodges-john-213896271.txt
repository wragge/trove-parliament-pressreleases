 ,MIGRANT PROJECTS RECEIVE FEDERAL FUNDS

 Funds for Tour projects assisting Queensland migrant and 

 ethnic organisations -were announced today by Minister for 

 Immigration and Ethnic Affairs, John Hodges.

 Mr Hodges said the grants were valued at $ "16 500.

 He said a total of 13 grants have been made under the Migrant 

 1  Project Subsidy-Scheme, valued at $57 780, for self-help 

 welfare projects in ethnic communities, Australia-wide.

 The four Queensland organisations are the Vietnamese Community 

 in Australia, Queensland chapter; Mackay South. Sea Islanders 

 Community,. Queensland division of Australian Red Cross and 

 Associazione Nazionale Famiglie Degli Emigrati (ANFE). '

 Mr Hodges said the Queensland Vietnamese Community will 

 receive $6500 for furnishing a cottage which will help refugee 

 youth without family to settle into the community.

 The Mackay ifouth Sea Islanders Community will receive $4000 for 

 raxv materials for furniture, audio-visual and other equipment for 

 the club. The group, Mr Hodges said, will build its own 

 furniture and the grant will also help extend services,

 especially for the children.

 The Queensland Red Cross will receive $3000 to buy a television· 

 set and other furniture for a corrmrunity room for newly arrived 

 migrants and refugees at Wacol· Hostel, Brisbane.

 ANFE will receive $3000 for kitchen' equipment for low cost .  

 functions to involve isolated women, the ageing and unemployed 

 youth in community activities.
