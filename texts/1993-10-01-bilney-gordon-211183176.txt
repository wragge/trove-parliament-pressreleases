 MDC 54 1 OCTOBER 1993AUSTRALIA INCREASES AID TO ERITREAAustralia will increase aid to Eritrea by 60 per cent to $6 million in 1993-94 the Minister for Development Cooperation, Gordon Bilney, said today."Eritrea, the world's youngest nation, needs international assistance to recover from a generation of war and concentrate on economic and social development," Mr Bilneysaid."Securing peace and independence was just the first step for Eritrea."Eritrea's needs are enormous and it will be some time before it can shift attention fromemergency relief to rehabilitation and development."Survival for nearly three quarters of the population is based on emergency relief fromdonor countries such as Australia."The provision of food aid to sustain its population will remain a major short term focusof international assistance."The pressures on already limited food and infrastructure resources are increasing as more than a quarter of the population are returning refugees or ex-fighters."Assistance with demobilisation of the fighters and assistance to rebuild Eritrea'sagricultural sector are obvious priorities."An Australian aid team will travel to Eritrea soon to assess the priority needs andmatch these with Australian expertise and capabilities."Australian non government organisations and prominent individuals, such as the late Fred Hollows and Thomas Keneally, have played a significant role in Eritrea during theconflict and will play an important role now that peace and independence have beensecured," Mr Bilney said.Funds will be provided by AIDAB, Australia's overseas aid agency.* * * * *CANBERRA[_COMMONWEALTH PARLIAMENTARY LIBRARY MICAHFor further information contact: Richard Moore (Minister's Office) 06 277 7660 David Jones (AIDAB) 06 276 4967 Fessahaie Abraham (Govt of Eritrea) 06 282 3489AH 281 4578AH 253 1229 AH 241 8097
